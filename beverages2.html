TEH TARIK

BACKGROUND

In Malay, teh literally means “tea” and tarik “pulled”. Composed of black tea, sugar and sweetened condensed milk, the main characteristic of teh tarik is the method of preparation. The drink is prepared using two cups and is transferred from one to the other until it takes on a velvety, creamy consistency, lowering the temperature and producing a characteristic foam on top. This blend is a source of pride in Malaysia and an icon of the local culture and the tea “dance” between the two cups is perfectly made by experts, the liquid taking off high without losing a drop.

INDREGIENTS

  • 2 ½ tbsp tea dust
  • 750 ml hot water
  • 4 tbsp condensed milk
  • 5 tbsp evaporated milk

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a saucepan, bring water to a boil, then leave to cool for a few minutes.
  2. Place tea dust in a cotton tea strainer over one metal mug, and add water.
  3. Let stand for 10-15 minutes before removing the strainer.
  4. In a separate metal mug, add condensed and evaporated milk.
  5. Carefully pour the two mixtures back and forth from the height of an arm’s length to create a thick froth.
  6. Once frothy and well mixed, pour the tea into two glasses and serve immediately.
